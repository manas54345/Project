Predicting Top 10 Playoff Performers 2019-2020


  1. Joel Embiid

    2. The Process is back in playoff contention and is looking to prove something after last year's run. Joel is averaging 23.4ppg, 11.8reb and 3.1 assists. While the 76ers haven't been that successful in the playoffs as of late, this season seems different as the sixers have added more depth with Horford and Richardson. Look for Embiid to lead this team.


  2. Nikola Jokic

    3. Jokic is by far one of the most versitile big men in the league. At 20.2ppg, 10.2reb and 6.9 assists, he has been improving his game vastly since 2016. I look forward to seeing Jokic and his abilities this season as the Nuggets look to make a deep playoffs push.


  3. Jimmy Butler

    4. Butler has proved that he is a leader. Taking his talents south, Jimmy has averaged 20.2ppg, 6.6reb and 6.1 assists. Jimmy has a young group which looks very promising. I think he will be able to make a run into the playoffs and become a serious contender in the East.


  4. Damian Lillard

    5. Coming off an amazing playoff series last year against OKC, Damian has proved to the NBA community that he is definitely a force in the playoffs. Lillard has been on a tear this season at 28.9ppg, 4.3reb and 7.8 assists. Portland has been able to make the playoffs the past couple seasons but have been unable to make a deep run in the playoffs. Maybe this year is different.


  5. Luka Doncic

    6. The wonder kid is on a tear this season. Luka Magic has lived up to his expectations and exceeded them by bounds and leaps. It's unheard of a 20 year old averaging 28.7ppg, 9.3reb and 8.7 assists in his second year in the NBA. Doncic has displayed immense growth and I look forward to seeing him in the playoffs. While his play looks amazing, he has a lot to prove in a much different environment; the NBA playoffs.


  6. Anthony Davis

    7. A force at both ends of the floor, Anthony Davis is the definition of a versitile big man. This season, AD has been averaging 26.7ppg, 9.4reb and 3.1 assists. AD now has a chance to make a deep run into the playoffs with a great Lakers team. The Lakers are the team to beat and Anthony Davis won't be playing around come playoffs basketball.


  7. James Harden

    8. A prolific scorer who is capable of dismantling any defence when he is at his best. Averaging 34.4ppg, 6.4reb and 7.4 assists, James Harden is continuing where he left off last season. While his regular season stats are unreal, we haven't quite seen that performance transfer over into the playoff environment. I don't think the Rockets wil be able to make a deep run without a proper center and two ball dominant players in James Harden and Russell Westbrook.


  8. Giannis Antetokounmpo

    9. After a MVP season last year, Giannis is looking to estabilish himself as much more this season. Currently, Giannis is averaging 29.6 ppg, 13.7 reb and 5.8 assists; making a case for MVP and DPOY. Giannis' contribution has led to the Bucks into the top spot in the east. His efforts are unignorable but is lack of playoff prowess denies him the top spot on this list. Will Giannis finally be able to make it out of the East without Kawhi Leonard in his way?


  9. Kawhi Leonard

    10. Coming off an NBA Championship, Kawhi is definitely a top three pick. Now in the West, Kawhi will have to face much tougher competition than the East. During the regular season, Leonard has averaged 26.9ppg, 7.3 reb and 5 assists. While Kawhi's individual numbers are outstanding, the lack of playing time with injury-prone Paul George may cause some issues come playoff time.


  10. Lebron James

    11. A perennial All-Star with consistent stats, LeBron James is the top pick this year. While in the regular season, there are many talented players, the playoffs reveal the cream of the crop. LeBron is averaging 25.7ppg, 7.9 reb and a career high 10.6 assists per game. It's safe to say LeBron is still a strong force within the league. Along with Anthony Davis, the Lakers will be a tough team to overcome.
